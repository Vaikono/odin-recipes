Monte Cristo Sandwich Recipe

According to Wikipedia, A Monte Cristo sandwich is an egg-dipped or batter-dipped ham and cheese sandwich that is pan or deep fried. It is a variation of the French croque monsieur. From the 1930s to the 1960s, American cookbooks had recipes under such names as "French sandwich", "toasted ham sandwich", and "French toasted cheese sandwich".

On this page, you will learn how to make a Monte Cristo sandwich.

Ingredient:

  • 1 quart oil for frying, or as needed
  • ⅔ cup water
  • 1 egg
  • ⅔ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 ¾ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 8 slices white bread
  • 4 slices Swiss cheese
  • 4 slices turkey
  • 4 slices ham
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon confectioners' sugar for dusting

Steps:

  1. Heat 5 inches of oil in a deep-fryer to 365 degree
  2. While oil is heating, make the batter in medium bowl by whisking the egg and water
  3. Combine the flour, baking powder, salt and pepper with the batter
  4. Whisk the egg mixture until smooth.
  5. Set the batter aside in the refrigerator
  6. Assemble sandwiches by placing one slice of turkey on one slice of bread, a slice of ham on another, then sandwich them with the Swiss cheese in the middle.
  7. Cut the sandwiches into quarters and secure with toothpicks
  8. Dip each sandwich quarter in the batter with all sides coated
  9. Deep fry in the hot oil until golden brown on all sides
  10. Remove toothpicks and arrange on a serving tray
  11. Dust the sandwiches with confectioners' sugar prior to serving
  12. Enjoy!
